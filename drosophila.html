Regenerating Fly Limbs Using Genetic Engineering

Goentoro Lab - California Institute of Technology, Summer 2024

Numerous biological mechanisms control the growth and development of Drosophila Melanogaster flies. During my time with the Goentoro Lab group, I investigated if it was possible to utlilize genetics to accelerate a flyâ€™s metabolic pathway and produce a regenerative response.

This project experimented with two genetically altered fly lines: r4>TOR and Dilp2-Gal4>UAS-NaChBac, which affected the liver and brain respectively.

The r4>TOR line showed statistically signifigant regenerative potential.

The Dilp2-Gal4>UAS-NaChBac line did not have signifigant data, but yieded a outlier showing 40% regeneration.
